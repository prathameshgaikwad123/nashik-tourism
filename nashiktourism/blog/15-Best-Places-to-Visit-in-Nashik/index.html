15 Best Places to Visit in Nashik
2026 Guide + Hidden Gems

Temples, vineyards, caves and hidden gems — everything you need to plan your Nashik trip.

Travel Guide May 2026 10 min read

Planning a trip and wondering what to actually see in Nashik?

Most guides repeat the same places. This one doesn’t.

This is a real, experience-based list—covering temples, vineyards, caves, and hidden spots—so you can plan your trip without confusion.

Top Places to Visit in Nashik

1. Trimbakeshwar Temple

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, this is the spiritual heart of Nashik.

  • Deep religious significance
  • Unique Shiva linga (three-faced form)
  • Strong connection to Kumbh Mela

2. Ramkund

A sacred bathing ghat on the Godavari River.

  • Central location during Kumbh Mela
  • Peaceful spiritual vibe
  • Evening aarti experience

3. Sula Vineyards

The reason Nashik is called the Wine Capital of India.

  • Wine tasting tours
  • Scenic vineyard views
  • Great for couples & friends

4. Pandavleni Caves

Ancient Buddhist caves carved into rock.

  • History + trekking combo
  • Amazing city views
  • Less crowded

5. Anjneri Hill

Believed to be the birthplace of Lord Hanuman.

6. Brahmagiri Hills

Source of the Godavari River.

7. Kalaram Temple

Famous for its black stone idol of Lord Rama.

8. Sita Gufa

A small cave linked to the Ramayana.

9. Dudhsagar Falls

Best during monsoon for nature lovers.

10. Someshwar Temple

A peaceful temple near a flowing river.

11. Harihar Fort

Famous for its near-vertical stairs.

12. Saraf Bazaar

Perfect for local shopping.

13. York Winery

Less crowded alternative to Sula.

14. Gangapur Dam

Great for sunsets and relaxed evenings.

15. Vaitarna Lake

A hidden gem near Nashik.

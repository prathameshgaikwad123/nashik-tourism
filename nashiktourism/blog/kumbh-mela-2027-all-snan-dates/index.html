Kumbh Mela 2027
All Snan Dates — Complete Calendar

Every auspicious bathing date for Nashik and Trimbakeshwar — from Amrit Snan to all 45+ Parva Snan dates.

Kumbh MelaApril 202610 min read

One of the biggest misconceptions about Kumbh Mela 2027 is that there are only 3 important bathing dates. In reality, the Simhastha Kumbh Mela in Nashik has over 45 auspicious bathing dates spread across the full Mela period. The government specifically extended this Kumbh across 21 months to reduce crowd pressure and allow more pilgrims to participate on less crowded days.

💡 Key Insight

You do not need to come on Amrit Snan days to receive full spiritual benefit. Any Parva Snan (auspicious bathing date) is considered holy and blessed. Visiting on a non-peak date means shorter queues, better access to the ghat, easier accommodation and significantly lower costs.

The 3 Most Important Dates — Amrit Snan

These are the most crowded and spiritually significant days. The 13 Akharas lead grand processions to the ghat on these dates:

Which Date Should You Choose?

For Maximum Spiritual Significance — Amrit Snan Days

August 2, August 31, and September 11–12, 2027. These are the days when the Akharas lead processions, the energy is at its peak, and the spiritual atmosphere is unmatched. Expect massive crowds — arrive the night before and be at the ghat by 4 AM.

For a Peaceful Experience — Parva Snan Days

Any of the Parva Snan dates in August or September 2027 offer excellent spiritual experience with far smaller crowds. Guru Purnima (18 July), Shravan Purnima/Raksha Bandhan (17 August) and Kartik Purnima (31 October 2027) are particularly auspicious.

For Pilgrims Who Cannot Travel in Peak Months

The Mela runs until July 2028. Kartik month (October–November 2027) is considered especially sacred for bathing in any tirtha. Visiting during this period offers a spiritual Kumbh experience with practical convenience.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many bathing dates are there in Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027?

There are over 45 auspicious bathing dates across Nashik and Trimbakeshwar during the full Kumbh Mela period (October 2026 to July 2028). The three Amrit Snan dates are the most significant, but all Parva Snan dates are equally blessed for devotees.

Can I visit Kumbh Mela 2027 after September 2027?

Yes. The Mela continues until July 24, 2028 with regular bathing dates. Kartik month (October–November 2027) and Makar Sankranti (January 2028) are particularly significant Parva Snan periods after the main Amrit Snan dates.

What is the difference between Amrit Snan and Parva Snan?

Amrit Snan (formerly Shahi Snan) are the three main bathing dates when the 13 Akharas lead sacred processions. Parva Snan are the other auspicious bathing dates based on Hindu calendar events like Purnima, Amavasya, Ekadashi and Sankranti. Both are considered holy and blessed.

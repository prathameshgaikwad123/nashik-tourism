Sula Vineyards Nashik
Complete Visit Guide

India's wine capital. Award-winning wines, vineyard tours, resort stay and the famous SulaFest — all in the Sahyadri foothills near Nashik.

Nashik GuidesApril 20268 min read

Sula Vineyards is the name that put Nashik on India's tourism map independent of its religious significance. Founded in 1999 by Rajeev Samant, Sula has grown into India's largest wine producer and a destination in its own right — attracting over 3 lakh visitors annually to its estate in the Sahyadri foothills near Nashik.

📅 Quick Facts

Location: Govardi village, Gangapur Road, Nashik • Distance: 15 km from Nashik city • Open: Mon–Sun 11 AM–10 PM • Wine tasting from: ₹300 per person

What to Do at Sula Vineyards

Wine Tasting (La Cave Tasting Room)

Sula's tasting room overlooks the vineyards and offers structured sessions guided by a sommelier. Packages range from a 3-wine flight (₹300) to an 8-wine premium experience (₹800). You'll taste the Sauvignon Blanc, Dindori Reserve Shiraz, sparkling Brut and their dessert Madera wine. Book in advance on weekends at sulavineyards.com.

Vineyard Walk

Guided walks through rows of Cabernet Sauvignon, Shiraz and Sauvignon Blanc vines. Best October–February when vines are lush. During harvest (January–March) you can watch the picking process firsthand.

Dining

Little Italy — Italian restaurant, lunch and dinner, ₹600–1,200 per person. Soma wine bar — lighter meals and wine by the glass with vineyard views. Both excellent for pairing local wine with food.

Resort Stay — Beyond by Sula

Boutique resort directly on the vineyard estate. Rooms and villas with vineyard views. Rates: ₹7,000–15,000 per night. Book 4–6 weeks ahead for weekends.

SulaFest

India's premier wine and music festival, held annually in February. Live artists, wine tasting, food stalls across two days. Tickets ₹2,000–5,000 per day. Exact 2027 dates TBD — typically first or second weekend of February.

Sula Wines to Know Before You Visit

  • The Source Sauvignon Blanc — Their flagship white. Crisp, tropical, India's best-selling Sauvignon Blanc.
  • Dindori Reserve Shiraz — Premium red. Named after a Nashik region. Full-bodied, complex. Best Shiraz made in India.
  • Rasa Cabernet Sauvignon — Earthy, well-structured premium red.
  • Brut — Sparkling wine, methode traditionelle. Genuinely good.
  • Madera NV — Port-style dessert wine. Unique and excellent after dinner.

How to Reach Sula Vineyards from Nashik

  • Ola/Uber cab: ₹200–300 from city centre, 20–25 minutes. Most reliable.
  • Auto-rickshaw: ₹100–150. May need to negotiate.
  • Self drive: Take Gangapur Road from Nashik. Google Maps "Sula Vineyards Nashik" is accurate.

Best Time to Visit Sula Vineyards

November–March is ideal — cool weather, green vines, harvest activity January–March. July–September (Monsoon) — lush and beautiful, tasting continues indoors. If you're in Nashik for Kumbh Mela 2027, a monsoon visit to Sula is a memorable contrast to the ghat experience. Weekday mornings are always quietest regardless of season.

Visiting Sula Alongside Kumbh Mela 2027

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to book in advance for Sula Vineyards?

Weekday walk-ins are usually fine. Weekends and SulaFest require advance booking via sulavineyards.com.

Is Sula suitable for non-drinkers?

Yes. The vineyard walk, restaurant and resort are all enjoyable without wine tasting. Many families visit purely for the setting.

What is the entry fee?

No entry fee to the estate. Wine tasting packages start at ₹300 per person.

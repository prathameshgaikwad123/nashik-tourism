Budget Guide — How Much Do You Need?

Honest cost breakdown for budget, mid-range and comfortable travellers. From Mumbai, Pune and other cities.

PlanningApril 20268 min read

The most common question from first-time Kumbh Mela planners: how much money should I bring? The honest answer — it depends entirely on your travel style and city of origin. But here is a clear, honest breakdown that you can use to plan your actual budget, not a generic estimate.

⚠️ Important: Surge Pricing During Amrit Snan

Hotels, private transport and some food vendors will charge 2–5x normal prices on and around the three Amrit Snan dates (Aug 2, Aug 31, Sep 11–12, 2027). The prices below are for normal Kumbh period days. If visiting on Amrit Snan dates, add 50–200% to accommodation and transport estimates.

Money Tips for Kumbh Mela

  • Carry cash — ATMs near the ghat will have queues of 30–60 minutes on peak days. Withdraw before arriving.
  • UPI works fine — Most food vendors and shops accept Google Pay / PhonePe. Carry both cash and UPI.
  • Book transport early — Train prices on Amrit Snan dates (if seats are even available) will be at premium Tatkal rates. Book 60–90 days ahead.
  • Negotiate auto fares — During Kumbh, auto drivers will quote inflated rates. Agree on the fare before getting in.
  • Free food at langars — Multiple Sikh Gurudwaras and religious organizations run free community kitchens near the ghats. Clean, vegetarian, no expectation to pay.
  • Avoid peak day surge — Visit on a Parva Snan date (not Amrit Snan) to cut accommodation costs by 50–70%.

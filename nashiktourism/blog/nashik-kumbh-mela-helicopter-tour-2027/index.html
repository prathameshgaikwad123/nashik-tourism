Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027
Helicopter Tour — Pilgrimage Circuit Guide

The complete guide to helicopter pilgrimage services for Kumbh 2027 — Nashik to Trimbakeshwar, Shirdi and Shani Shingnapur. Routes, costs and how to book.

Kumbh MelaApril 20268 min read

One of the most exciting developments for Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027 is the planned helicopter pilgrimage circuit connecting Nashik's sacred sites with other major Maharashtra pilgrimage destinations. For elderly pilgrims, devotees with mobility challenges or those wanting to cover multiple sacred sites in a single day, this service offers something that simply did not exist at the previous Nashik Kumbh. This guide covers everything confirmed and expected about the 2027 helicopter services.

📅 Status — April 2026

Helicopter pilgrimage services for Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027 are in the planning stage. The Maharashtra Tourism Development Corporation (MTDC) and private operators have indicated interest, with bookings expected to open approximately 6 months before the Mela (around early 2027). This page will be updated with booking links when they go live.

What Is the Nashik Kumbh Mela Helicopter Pilgrimage Circuit?

The proposed heli-circuit for Kumbh 2027 is a helicopter tour service that connects Nashik's Kumbh Mela sites with nearby pilgrimage destinations by air. The concept mirrors the successful Chardham helicopter services in Uttarakhand and the Shirdi heli-services that already operate in Maharashtra.

The planned circuit covers:

  • Nashik (Ramkund / Gangapur area) — Primary Godavari bathing site, Kumbh Mela centre
  • Trimbakeshwar — Jyotirlinga temple, Kushavarta Kund, 28 km from Nashik by road (10 min by helicopter)
  • Shirdi — Sai Baba shrine, one of Maharashtra's busiest pilgrimage sites, approximately 80 km from Nashik
  • Shani Shingnapur — Renowned Shani (Saturn deity) temple, approximately 100 km from Nashik
  • Pandharpur (seasonal) — Vithoba temple on the Bhima river — possible extension

Why the Helicopter Circuit Matters for Kumbh 2027

The Nashik Kumbh 2027 is unique because it spans two sacred sites 28 km apart — Ramkund in Nashik and Kushavarta Kund in Trimbakeshwar. For most pilgrims, travelling between the two by road on peak days (when roads are severely congested) is extremely challenging. A helicopter service would allow:

  • Elderly devotees to bathe at both ghats — Ramkund and Kushavarta — in a single morning
  • Pilgrims to combine the Kumbh with Shirdi and Shani Shingnapur darshan in one day
  • Groups to avoid road congestion entirely on Amrit Snan days
  • International visitors to see Maharashtra's pilgrimage landscape from the air

Expected Routes and Timings

Based on MTDC announcements and similar services at Prayagraj Kumbh 2025, here is what to expect:

  • Nashik ↔ Trimbakeshwar: ~10 minutes flight. Multiple departures per day. This is the most likely route to be confirmed first.
  • Nashik ↔ Shirdi: ~30 minutes flight. Daily service. This route may operate independently of Kumbh as Shirdi already has helicopter connectivity.
  • Nashik ↔ Shani Shingnapur: ~25 minutes flight. Likely part of a combined circuit.
  • Full circuit (Nashik → Trimbakeshwar → Shirdi → Shani Shingnapur → Nashik): Approximately half day.

Expected Costs

Based on comparable helicopter pilgrimage services currently operating in Maharashtra and Uttarakhand:

  • Nashik ↔ Trimbakeshwar: ₹3,000 – ₹6,000 per person (one way)
  • Nashik ↔ Shirdi: ₹5,000 – ₹10,000 per person (one way)
  • Full circuit: ₹15,000 – ₹30,000 per person
  • Prices will be confirmed when bookings open — expect Kumbh period pricing to be higher than off-peak rates

Who Will Operate the Service?

Helicopter services in Maharashtra are typically operated by DGCA-registered private operators in partnership with MTDC. Operators who currently run services in this region include Deccan Charters and private charter companies based in Nashik and Mumbai. Bookings will likely be through:

  • MTDC's official website (maharashtratourism.gov.in)
  • The official Kumbh Mela 2027 portal (expected: simhastha2027.maharashtra.gov.in)
  • Third-party travel platforms like MakeMyTrip, Yatra and Cleartrip (if partnered)

Is a Helicopter Service Worth It for You?

Honest assessment:

  • Yes — if you are elderly or have mobility challenges: The helicopter eliminates the ordeal of Amrit Snan day road congestion and the long walk to the ghat. For senior pilgrims, this transforms a potentially very difficult experience into a manageable one.
  • Yes — if you want to visit both Nashik and Trimbakeshwar on the same day: Road travel between the two on peak days can take 3+ hours each way. Helicopter does it in 10 minutes.
  • Yes — if combining with Shirdi: The Nashik–Shirdi–Shani Shingnapur circuit by road is a full day trip. By helicopter it becomes a half-day, leaving the afternoon free.
  • Not necessary — if you are a young, mobile pilgrim: Road transport between Nashik and Trimbakeshwar by shared jeep (₹50–80) or private cab (₹400–600) is perfectly practical on non-peak days. The helicopter cost is 50–100x more.

Alternative Air View Options

If the helicopter circuit is too expensive or not bookable, there is one alternative: seaplane services on the Godavari were discussed in early NTKMA planning documents, though as of April 2026 this has not been officially confirmed. Watch for announcements from MTDC.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a helicopter service for Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027?

Services are planned and being arranged by MTDC and private operators. Bookings are expected to open approximately 6 months before the Mela (early 2027). We will update this page with official booking links when they go live.

Can elderly pilgrims use the helicopter service?

Yes — this is specifically one of the primary reasons the service is being introduced. Standard helicopter seating accommodates passengers with limited mobility. Check with the specific operator about any physical requirements when booking.

How do I book a helicopter for Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027?

Once available, bookings will be through MTDC's official website, the official Kumbh portal and potentially through major travel platforms. We will publish the direct booking link on this page the moment it is available.

Is there a helicopter between Nashik and Trimbakeshwar?

This is the most likely route to be confirmed — 10 minutes flight versus 45–60 minutes by road (and 3+ hours on peak Kumbh days). Exact pricing and frequency will be announced closer to the Mela.

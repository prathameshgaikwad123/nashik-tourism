Ramkund Ghat Nashik
Complete Guide — Timings, History & Kumbh 2027

The sacred bathing ghat on the Godavari river. Heart of Nashik spirituality and Kumbh Mela 2027 site.

Nashik GuidesApril 20267 min read

Ramkund is the most sacred bathing ghat in Nashik and the primary site for the Kumbh Mela 2027. Located in the Panchavati area on the banks of the Godavari river, it draws hundreds of thousands of pilgrims daily during Kumbh season and thousands year-round. This is where Lord Rama is believed to have bathed during his 14-year exile, making the water here among the most spiritually significant in all of India.

History and Significance of Ramkund

Ramkund is not merely a bathing ghat — it is one of the most spiritually charged sites in the Hindu tradition. According to the Valmiki Ramayana, Lord Rama along with Sita and Lakshmana settled in the Panchavati forest near Nashik during their 14 years of Vanvas (exile). Ramkund is the spot where Lord Rama bathed in the Godavari. The word "Kund" means a sacred water body, and "Ram" refers to Lord Rama himself.

The ghat gained its current structure during the Peshwa era in the 18th century, with stone steps leading down to the river. The site has been a centre of pilgrimage for over 500 years and hosts the Kumbh Mela every 12 years when it becomes the largest religious gathering site in Maharashtra.

Religiously, bathing at Ramkund is believed to wash away sins (papa), help ancestors attain mukti (liberation), and bring spiritual blessings. This is why the ghat is also used for the immersion of ashes (asthi visarjan) — the last rites for the deceased — as it is believed the Godavari carries the soul to liberation.

🌟 Best Time to Visit Ramkund

The morning aarti between 6–7 AM is the most spiritually charged and visually spectacular time to visit. The evening aarti (7–8 PM) with diyas on the river is equally beautiful and less crowded than morning. Avoid 8–10 AM on weekends — these are the most crowded non-Kumbh hours.

How to Reach Ramkund Ghat

From Nashik Road Railway Station

Nashik Road Railway Station is approximately 8 km from Ramkund. Take an auto-rickshaw (₹60–100) or taxi (₹150–200). Journey time: 20–30 minutes in normal traffic. During Kumbh peak days, roads near the ghat are restricted — you will need to walk the last 1–2 km from a designated parking zone.

From Nashik City Centre (CBS Bus Stand)

Panchavati area and Ramkund are in the same zone. Auto-rickshaw from CBS: ₹30–60. Journey: 10–15 minutes. Local MSRTC city buses also run to Panchavati directly.

From Trimbakeshwar

Trimbakeshwar is 28 km from Ramkund. Shared jeeps run regularly between the two — ₹50–80 per person. Private taxi: ₹400–600 one way. Journey: 45–60 minutes depending on traffic.

By Walking (from Panchavati)

If you are staying in the Panchavati area, Ramkund is likely within walking distance — 5–20 minutes depending on your hotel. Ask your hotel for the exact walking route. The lanes near Ramkund are narrow and best navigated on foot.

Rules at Ramkund Ghat

  • Photography: Allowed in general areas. Do not photograph other bathers without permission.
  • Clothing: You must wear clothing to enter the ghat. Changing rooms are available nearby.
  • Footwear: Remove footwear at the top of the ghat steps before descending.
  • Plastic: Strictly no single-use plastic near the river.
  • Food: No eating at the ghat steps itself.
  • Loud music / speakers: Not permitted at the ghat.
  • Immersion of idols: Regulated — check with local authorities during festivals.
  • Asthi visarjan (ash immersion): Permitted with prior contact with local priests (pandas).

What to See Near Ramkund

Ramkund sits in the heart of the Panchavati area, surrounded by temples and sacred sites within easy walking distance:

  • Kalaram Temple (500m) — Famous black Ram idol, one of Nashik's most important temples
  • Sita Gufa (1 km) — The cave where Sita is believed to have stayed during Vanvas
  • Panchavati Banyan Tree (200m) — The ancient fig grove referenced in the Ramayana
  • Ganga Ghat (adjacent) — Secondary ghat on the Godavari next to Ramkund
  • Muktidham Temple (3 km) — White marble temple featuring replicas of India's 12 Jyotirlingas
  • Coin Museum (4 km) — One of India's finest numismatic collections

Ramkund During Kumbh Mela 2027

During the Kumbh Mela 2027, Ramkund will be the centre of the entire event for Vaishnava pilgrims. Here is what changes during Kumbh:

  • Ghat access extended from 3 AM on Amrit Snan days
  • All roads within 500m restricted to foot traffic on peak days
  • Additional ghats opened along the Godavari to reduce crowd pressure at Ramkund
  • Police and volunteer presence at every ghat step
  • Medical camps set up within 200m of the ghat
  • Crowd control corridors established — enter and exit are separated
  • CCTV monitoring throughout the ghat area

The three Amrit Snan dates at Ramkund are August 2, August 31, and September 11, 2027. On these days, the Vaishnava Akharas lead grand processions from their camps to the ghat — arriving as early as 5 AM. General public bathing opens after the Akharas complete their sacred dip.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ramkund open every day?

Yes. Ramkund is open 365 days a year from approximately 5:30 AM to 9:30 PM. There are no holidays or closures except during extreme weather conditions.

What is the best time to avoid crowds at Ramkund?

Weekday afternoons between 12 PM and 4 PM are the quietest times. Early morning (before 7 AM) on weekdays is also relatively uncrowded. Avoid Sunday mornings — these are the busiest non-festival times.

Can non-Hindus visit Ramkund?

Yes. There is no religious restriction on who can visit or bathe at Ramkund. The ghat is open to all. International tourists regularly visit and are welcome.

Is there parking near Ramkund?

Limited parking is available in the Panchavati area. During Kumbh Mela, vehicles are not permitted within 1–2 km of the ghat on peak days. Walking from designated parking zones is necessary on Amrit Snan dates.

Sadhugram at Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027
Complete Visitor Guide

The sadhu village inside Kumbh Mela — what it is, where to find it, what happens inside and how any visitor can experience the most extraordinary part of the Kumbh.

Kumbh MelaApril 20268 min read

Millions of people attend Kumbh Mela for the holy bath. But far fewer know about Sadhugram — and those who do often describe it as the most extraordinary part of the entire Kumbh experience. This guide tells you exactly what Sadhugram is, where it is, what you will find inside and how visitors can access it during Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027.

What Is Sadhugram?

Sadhugram (literally "sadhu village") is the temporary township constructed specifically to house the 13 Akharas and their thousands of Naga Sadhus, saints, mahamandaleshwars and spiritual leaders during the Kumbh Mela period. It is one of the most extraordinary temporary settlements built anywhere in the world — a fully functional religious city that exists only during the Mela.

During Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027, Sadhugram will house tens of thousands of sadhus across a specially allocated zone near the Tapovan area of Nashik. Each Akhara gets a designated plot where they construct their camp — complete with temples, kitchens, dhuni fires, meditation spaces, dispensaries and accommodation for both sadhus and visiting devotees.

Where Is Sadhugram Located in Nashik?

For the 2027 Simhastha Kumbh Mela, the Sadhugram is being established in the Tapovan area on the banks of the Godavari river — approximately 2–3 km from Ramkund ghat in Panchavati. The NTKMA assigns specific plots to each of the 13 Akharas within the Sadhugram grounds. The area will be clearly signed during the Mela period.

Exact GPS coordinates and maps will be available through the official Kumbh Mela app (expected before October 31, 2026) and from the NTKMA visitor information centres that will operate near major entry points.

What Is Inside Sadhugram?

Each Akhara's camp within Sadhugram is essentially a self-contained religious village:

  • The Dhuni: A sacred fire that burns continuously throughout the Kumbh period. Naga Sadhus live and meditate around the dhuni. This is the spiritual heart of each camp.
  • The Mahamandaleshwar's quarters: The senior leadership of each Akhara resides in a more structured area. Devotees seek darshan (audience) with the mahamandaleshwar here.
  • Temple structures: Each Akhara constructs temporary temples dedicated to their primary deity.
  • Langar (community kitchen): Free meals are served to thousands of visitors daily from most Akhara camps. Simple vegetarian food — dal, rice, roti — available throughout the day.
  • Pravachan halls: Daily spiritual discourses by senior saints, open to all visitors.
  • Medical facilities: Basic dispensaries within each major Akhara camp.

Can Visitors Enter Sadhugram?

Yes — and you should. Sadhugram is not a restricted zone for sadhus only. It is open to the general public during daylight hours and visiting is completely free. Millions of pilgrims visit Sadhugram to:

  • Seek darshan (blessings) from senior saints and mahamandaleshwars
  • Witness Naga Sadhus in their natural daily life — meditating, practising yoga, discussing scriptures around the dhuni
  • Eat langar — free vegetarian meals served with remarkable warmth
  • Attend pravachan (discourse) sessions by renowned saints
  • Simply experience the atmosphere — incense, chanting, dhuni smoke, the sound of conch and drum
🌟 Why Sadhugram Is Worth Visiting

Many experienced Kumbh Mela pilgrims say that Sadhugram is more profound than the ghat itself. The ghat is extraordinary — but it is crowded and brief. Inside an Akhara camp in Sadhugram, you can sit quietly beside a dhuni fire, receive a blessing from a Naga Sadhu who has lived in the Himalayas for decades, and eat a meal prepared with devotion for thousands of strangers. This is the living tradition of India — unchanged for centuries.

Rules for Visiting Sadhugram

  • Dress modestly: Covered shoulders and legs. This is a deeply religious space.
  • Remove footwear when entering individual Akhara temple or dhuni areas — follow the cues of others around you
  • Photography: Ask permission before photographing any sadhu. Some camps may restrict photography in certain areas — look for signs.
  • Silence in meditation areas: Keep voices low near the dhuni and meditation spaces
  • Accept prasad with both hands: If offered food or prasad, receive it graciously
  • Do not touch or disturb Naga Sadhus who are in meditation
  • No alcohol or non-vegetarian food in or near Sadhugram
  • Follow escort instructions: Some Akhara areas require you to be accompanied by a camp volunteer — this is normal

Best Time to Visit Sadhugram During Kumbh 2027

  • Early morning (5–8 AM): The dhuni atmosphere is most powerful. Sadhus perform morning rituals. The light at this hour is extraordinary for those who want to photograph (with permission).
  • Mid-morning (9–11 AM): Langar begins at most camps. Pravachan sessions often start. Good time to receive blessings from saints.
  • Avoid Amrit Snan days: On August 2, August 31 and September 11–12, the Akharas are preparing for the procession from very early morning. Access to camps may be restricted.
  • Best days: Regular weekdays between Amrit Snan dates when sadhus are available, camps are open and the atmosphere is relaxed.

Which Akharas Are in Sadhugram at Nashik Kumbh 2027?

All 13 Akharas will have camps in Sadhugram during Nashik Kumbh 2027. The largest and most visited camps are typically those of:

  • Juna Akhara — Largest Shaiva Akhara, most Naga Sadhus, most active langar
  • Niranjani Akhara — Known for intellectual tradition, pravachans by senior scholars
  • Mahanirvani Akhara — Has strong Maharashtra connections, particularly accessible for Marathi-speaking pilgrims
  • Nirmohi Ani Akhara — Leading Vaishnava Akhara, beautifully decorated camp

How to Reach Sadhugram from Ramkund

Sadhugram in the Tapovan area is approximately 2–3 km from Ramkund ghat. Options:

  • Auto-rickshaw: ₹40–80 from Ramkund to Tapovan area. Ask specifically for "Sadhugram" — auto drivers know it well during Kumbh.
  • Walking: 25–35 minutes walk along the Godavari riverbank. Pleasant on non-peak days.
  • Kumbh shuttle buses: Government shuttle services are planned between major Kumbh zones including Sadhugram.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Sadhugram open to non-Hindus?

Yes. Sadhugram welcomes visitors of all backgrounds who come with respect and genuine curiosity. International tourists regularly visit and are warmly received at most Akhara camps.

Is the langar in Sadhugram really free?

Yes, completely. Langar at Akhara camps is given freely as a religious service — no payment expected or accepted. The food is simple, clean, vegetarian and prepared with devotion. Eating langar at Sadhugram is one of the most moving experiences of the Kumbh Mela.

How long should I spend in Sadhugram?

A minimum of 2 hours to walk through a few Akhara camps, eat langar and attend a portion of a pravachan. A half-day (4–5 hours) allows you to visit multiple camps, seek blessings from senior saints and absorb the atmosphere properly.

When exactly is Sadhugram established for Kumbh 2027?

Akharas begin setting up their Sadhugram camps approximately 1–2 months before the flag hoisting ceremony on October 31, 2026. The settlement is fully operational by the time the Kumbh officially begins and remains until the end of the Mela in July 2028.

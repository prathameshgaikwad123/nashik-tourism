Trimbakeshwar to Nashik
Distance, Route & Travel Guide

Everything you need to travel between the two Kumbh Mela sites — by shared jeep, cab, and bus.

Travel GuideApril 20265 min read

Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027 is unique among all Kumbh Melas — it is the only one celebrated at two separate sacred sites: Ramkund in Nashik city and Kushavarta Kund in Trimbakeshwar. Many pilgrims want to visit both. This guide tells you exactly how to travel between them, what it costs, and what to expect during the Kumbh period.

Distance from Trimbakeshwar to Nashik

The distance from Trimbakeshwar to Nashik city centre (Panchavati area) is approximately 28 kilometres via the main Nashik-Trimbak Road. The distance from Trimbakeshwar to Nashik Road Railway Station is approximately 35 km.

Shared Jeep (Recommended for Budget)

Shared jeeps are the most popular way to travel between Nashik and Trimbakeshwar. They depart regularly from the Nashik CBS (Central Bus Stand) area and from the Trimbakeshwar village centre. Jeeps wait until full (typically 8–10 passengers) and then depart. Frequency is very high — you rarely wait more than 15 minutes. Cost: ₹50–80 per person each way.

Private Cab (Ola, Uber or Local Taxi)

Private cabs offer door-to-door convenience. Book through Ola or Uber from Nashik city, or arrange with your hotel. Cost: ₹400–600 one way. Many pilgrims hire a cab for a full day (Nashik + Trimbakeshwar + return) for ₹1,500–2,500 — cost effective for families.

MSRTC State Bus

Government buses run regularly between Nashik CBS and Trimbakeshwar village. Cheaper than jeeps but slower with more stops. Cost: ₹30–50 per person. Bus frequency drops in evenings — plan to return by 6 PM if relying on state buses.

By Road (Self Drive)

The Nashik-Trimbak Road (Old Mumbai-Agra Road) is well maintained and clearly signed. Google Maps navigation works well on this route. Journey time is 35–45 minutes in normal traffic. During Kumbh, expect 90–120 minutes due to traffic restrictions near both sites on peak days.

The Route — What You Pass On the Way

The Nashik to Trimbakeshwar road passes through some beautiful Sahyadri terrain. Key landmarks on the route:

  • Gangapur Dam — 8 km from Nashik. The source of Nashik's water supply, with picturesque hills
  • Anjaneri Hill — 20 km from Nashik. Believed to be the birthplace of Lord Hanuman
  • Brahmagiri Mountain — visible as you approach Trimbakeshwar. Sacred peak and the true source of the Godavari
  • Trimbak village — the last 2 km, a traditional pilgrim town with hundreds of flower and puja stalls

During Kumbh Mela 2027 — What Changes

The Nashik–Trimbakeshwar route will be heavily managed during Kumbh Mela 2027, especially on the three Amrit Snan dates:

  • Traffic restrictions: Private vehicles are restricted within 2–3 km of Trimbakeshwar on Amrit Snan days. You will walk or take shuttle from parking zones.
  • Special Kumbh shuttles: Government will run dedicated shuttle buses between Nashik and Trimbakeshwar during the Mela period at subsidised rates.
  • Journey time doubles or triples: Normal 45-minute journey can take 2–3 hours on Amrit Snan days due to traffic volume. Plan accordingly.
  • Recommended strategy: Visit Trimbakeshwar on a different day from Nashik (Ramkund). Do not try to do both on an Amrit Snan date.
💡 Pro Tip for Kumbh

The Shaiva Akharas (followers of Lord Shiva) bathe at Kushavarta Kund in Trimbakeshwar. The Vaishnava Akharas bathe at Ramkund in Nashik. If you want to witness both Akhara processions, visit Nashik on September 11, 2027 and Trimbakeshwar on September 12, 2027 — the 3rd Amrit Snan is split across both sites on consecutive days.

Which Should You Visit First — Nashik or Trimbakeshwar?

If you are visiting both, the typical pilgrim route is: Trimbakeshwar first, then Nashik. Trimbakeshwar is quieter and more intimate. The Jyotirlinga temple there is deeply sacred. After the spiritual intensity of Trimbakeshwar, the Ramkund area in Nashik (which is more urban and crowded) feels like a natural progression.

However if you are arriving by train to Nashik Road station, it makes logistical sense to visit Nashik first, then take a shared jeep to Trimbakeshwar, and return to Nashik for your onward journey.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Trimbakeshwar from Nashik?

Trimbakeshwar is approximately 28 km from Nashik city centre (Panchavati area) and 35 km from Nashik Road Railway Station.

How long does it take to go from Nashik to Trimbakeshwar?

45–60 minutes by shared jeep or private cab in normal conditions. During Kumbh Mela 2027, expect 90–180 minutes on peak days.

What is the cheapest way to get from Nashik to Trimbakeshwar?

MSRTC state bus at ₹30–50 per person. Shared jeep from CBS at ₹50–80 per person is slightly more expensive but faster and more frequent.

Is there a train between Nashik and Trimbakeshwar?

No direct train. The nearest railway station to Trimbakeshwar is Nashik Road, from which you need road transport for the remaining 35 km.

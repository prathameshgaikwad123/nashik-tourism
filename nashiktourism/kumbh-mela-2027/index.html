Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027
Simhastha — Complete Guide

Official dates, Amrit Snan schedule, how to reach, where to stay — everything for pilgrims and travellers.

What is the Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027?

The Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027, also known as Simhastha Kumbh, is one of the four sacred Kumbh Melas held in India every 12 years. It takes place on the banks of the holy Godavari river in Nashik and at the Kushavarta Kund in Trimbakeshwar — home of the Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga.

The event is called "Simhastha" because it occurs when planet Jupiter (Guru/Brihaspati) enters the Leo zodiac sign (Simha Rashi). According to Hindu mythology, drops of the divine nectar (Amrit) that fell at this spot make bathing in the Godavari during this period spiritually transformative — capable of washing away lifetimes of karma.

Key fact: Between 15 to 20 crore (150–200 million) devotees are expected to participate in Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027, making it one of the largest human gatherings on earth.

Official Amrit Snan Dates 2027

The three main bathing dates — called Amrit Snan (formerly Shahi Snan) — are the most spiritually significant days. These are when the 13 Akharas lead processions to the river and millions of devotees follow.

Full Mela Duration: The Simhastha Kumbh Mela officially begins on 31 October 2026 (flag hoisting at Trimbakeshwar, Ramkund and Panchavati) and concludes on 24 July 2028. There are over 45 auspicious bathing dates across the full period — you don't have to come only on Amrit Snan days.

Two Sacred Locations — Nashik & Trimbakeshwar

Unlike other Kumbh Melas which have one primary bathing site, the Nashik Kumbh uniquely spans two locations 28 km apart:

Ramkund, Nashik (Panchavati)

Primary bathing ghat for Vaishnava Akharas (followers of Lord Vishnu/Ram). Lord Rama is believed to have bathed here during his exile. Located in the heart of Panchavati area.

Kushavarta Kund, Trimbakeshwar

Sacred bathing tank for Shaiva Akharas (followers of Lord Shiva). Located at the source of the Godavari river, near the Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga temple.

How to Reach Nashik for Kumbh Mela 2027

By Train

Nashik Road Railway Station is the main station, well connected to Mumbai (2.5 hrs), Pune (4 hrs), Delhi, Hyderabad and all major cities. During Kumbh, special trains will be added. Book tickets 60–90 days in advance — they sell out fast during Amrit Snan dates.

By Road (Bus)

MSRTC runs regular buses from Mumbai (3 hrs), Pune (4 hrs), Aurangabad (3 hrs) and other Maharashtra cities. Private bus operators also serve routes from Gujarat and other states. The Mumbai–Nashik Expressway (NH-3) makes the drive comfortable.

By Air

Ozar Airport (IATA: ISK) is 25 km from Nashik city. Flights connect to Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, and Hyderabad. Mumbai's Chhatrapati Shivaji International Airport (2.5 hrs by road) remains a major hub for those flying in from overseas.

Where to Stay in Nashik During Kumbh Mela

Accommodation near Ramkund and Trimbakeshwar will be in very high demand during Amrit Snan dates. Book at least 6 months in advance for the peak days.

  • Luxury hotels: Radisson Blu, Ibis Nashik, Express Inn — book via MakeMyTrip or directly
  • Mid-range hotels: Plenty of options in Panchavati area, 10–15 min walk from Ramkund
  • Dharamshalas: Many religious trusts run affordable dharamshalas near the ghats — contact local temples
  • Sadhugram: The government sets up temporary accommodation camps for pilgrims during the Mela
  • Trimbakeshwar: Stay closer to Kushavarta Kund if you're attending Shaiva Akhara events

What to Expect During Amrit Snan

Amrit Snan days are extraordinary experiences — but they require preparation:

  • Arrive the night before or very early morning (3–4 AM) to get close to the ghat
  • Akhara processions begin around dawn — a spectacular sight of Naga Sadhus and saints
  • General public bathing opens after the Akharas complete their snan
  • Crowd management will be significantly improved for 2027 (lessons from Prayagraj 2025)
  • Carry minimal belongings, wear simple clothes you can bathe in
  • Keep emergency contacts and a meeting point pre-decided with your group

Other Important Dates — Beyond Amrit Snan

You don't have to come only on the three main Amrit Snan dates. The government has encouraged spreading the visits across over 45 auspicious Parva Snan dates throughout the Mela period. Key ones include:

  • 29 July 2027 — Nagar Pradakshina (sacred circumambulation of Nashik city)
  • 18 July 2027 — Guru Purnima / Ashadha Purnima — 2nd day of Kumbha Yoga
  • Shravan Purnima, Bhadrapad Ekadashi, and many more auspicious dates through 2027–28

Nashik Kumbh Mela 2027 — Frequently Asked Questions

Is entry to Kumbh Mela free?

Yes. There is no registration fee or entry charge to attend the Kumbh Mela. Anyone can come and participate in the holy bath.

Is it safe to attend Kumbh Mela?

The Maharashtra government has committed ₹4,000 crores to infrastructure, crowd management, and safety improvements. The Mela has also been extended over a longer period specifically to reduce dangerous overcrowding on peak days.

What is the difference between Nashik and Prayagraj Kumbh?

Prayagraj (Maha Kumbh) is the largest Kumbh Mela. Nashik's Simhastha Kumbh is smaller but equally sacred, uniquely spanning two sites (Nashik + Trimbakeshwar) and occurring when Jupiter enters Leo — a specific astronomical configuration.

Can foreigners attend Nashik Kumbh Mela?

Yes. There are no restrictions. Foreign visitors require a valid Indian visa. Nashik is accessible via Mumbai airport (2.5 hrs by road).
